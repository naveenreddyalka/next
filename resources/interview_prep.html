Interview Prep Plan

6-week prep timeline · System design topics · Behavioral stories · Algo focus areas

6-week prep timeline

Week 1–2LeetCode foundation: Arrays, strings, hashmaps, two pointersComplete NeetCode.io Blind 75 list. Target: 2 Medium per day. Use Java or Scala (both are fine). Focus on pattern recognition, not memorization.
Week 3LeetCode advanced: Trees, graphs, BFS/DFS, dynamic programmingThese are the most commonly asked at Anthropic and Google. Pay special attention to graph problems — service topology questions appear in system design rounds too.
Week 4System design: Distributed systems fundamentalsMaster: (1) Design a distributed rate limiter, (2) Design a service registry with health checks, (3) Design a pub/sub system (you know Kafka — explain the internals), (4) Design an auth system with RBAC. Use Excalidraw to practice drawing architectures.
Week 5System design: AI/platform-specificFor Anthropic: Design a deployment pipeline for ML models. For ServiceNow: Design a multi-tenant SaaS platform. For NVIDIA: Design a multi-datacenter GPU job scheduler. For Databricks: Design a distributed data processing pipeline.
Week 6Mock interviews + behavioral prepDo 3–5 mock interviews on Pramp.com or with a friend. Polish your STAR stories (see below). Research each company's engineering blog.

System design: must-know topics for your targets

All companies: Consistent hashing + distributed cachingKnow how Cassandra, Redis, and consistent hashing work. You've used both in production — be ready to explain implementation tradeoffs, not just usage.
Anthropic/ServiceNow: Service mesh + observabilityKnow: distributed tracing (Jaeger/Zipkin), metrics collection (Prometheus/Grafana), log aggregation (ELK stack). Be able to design a full observability system from scratch.
Stripe: Payments reliability patternsKnow: idempotency keys, exactly-once delivery, saga pattern for distributed transactions, database sharding for payments. You have production experience — frame it architecturally.
Databricks/Confluent: Stream processingKnow: Kafka consumer groups, partition rebalancing, exactly-once semantics, Flink vs Spark Streaming tradeoffs. You've used Kafka at 5 companies — be the expert in the room.

Behavioral stories — prepare these (STAR format)

Q: "Tell me about a time you improved system performance significantly."
Use: MakeMyTrip — redesigned payment system, cut response times 50%, reduced maintenance cost 60%, improved success rates increasing revenue by millions. Be quantitative.
Q: "Tell me about owning a complex platform used by many teams."
Use: CreditKarma — own Finagle-based web framework used by ALL product development teams. Balancing framework stability vs team feature requests. Managing EOL technology.
Q: "Tell me about a system you designed from scratch."
Use: MakeMyTrip — total revamp of payment system, multi-datacenter, event-driven retry logic, Spring Boot + JPA + CouchBase + RabbitMQ. Explain architecture decisions.
Q: "Tell me about handling scale."
Use: Myntra/Flipkart — 500 million carts, hundreds of thousands of payments per day, 100% uptime requirement, in-house distributed database sharding over MySQL + Cassandra.
Q: "Tell me about working with legacy systems."
Use: CreditKarma — Finagle framework (Twitter, EOL), maintaining it for all product teams while contributing to open-source alternatives. Shows pragmatism + technical depth.
