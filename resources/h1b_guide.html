New wage-weighted lottery rules · How to maximize your odds · Company-by-company sponsorship data

What changed in 2026

Wage-weighted lottery (effective Feb 27, 2026)USCIS now assigns more lottery entries to higher-wage roles. Level IV salaries ($200K+ in Bay Area) = 61% selection rate. Level I = 15%. This fundamentally changes strategy: targeting premium companies is now the BEST H1B strategy, not just the career strategy.
$100K supplemental fee for overseas hiresNew fee for employers sponsoring workers from outside the US. Since you're already in the US (at CreditKarma on existing status), this fee does NOT apply to you. This is a significant advantage — employers strongly prefer in-country candidates for H1B.
Your current status at CreditKarmaIf you're on H1B or OPT at CreditKarma, you can transfer your H1B status to a new employer without waiting for the lottery (H1B transfer). This is a critical advantage — clarify your current visa status to know if you need a new lottery cap or just a transfer.

Action items — H1B specific

Step 1: Determine your current statusH1B transfer vs new lottery cap changes everything. If you're already on H1B at CreditKarma → you only need an H1B transfer = no lottery, no waiting, apply any time. Confirm with an immigration attorney.
Step 2: Ask in the recruiter screen, not laterSay: "I'm currently on [H1B/OPT] and will need H1B transfer/sponsorship. Is that something your company supports for this role?" This filters out companies early and shows you're organized.
Step 3: Prefer companies with in-house immigration counselGoogle, NVIDIA, and ServiceNow all have dedicated immigration teams. Smaller companies like Anthropic use outside counsel — not a dealbreaker but ask about their track record.
Lottery cap timing (if applicable)If you need a new H1B cap (not a transfer): Registration window is typically March, lottery in April, start date October 1. Plan job search timeline accordingly — aim to get offers by Feb/March to maximize options.
